Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 19, 2026

1. Introduction

Another Door Inc. (the “Company”) values the privacy of users of JangoLog (the “App”). This Privacy Policy explains what information the App processes and how that information is used.

2. Information Collected and Stored Directly by the App

The App does not require sign-up, and it does not transmit users’ personal information to the Company’s own servers for storage or management. However, to provide App features, information entered by the user in the App (e.g., expense records, categories, fixed expenses, budgets, notification settings, statistics preferences, and App settings) may be stored locally on the user’s device.

  • Locally stored information (examples): expense records, category information, fixed expense and subscription entries, budget settings, notification settings, currency/language/theme settings, and statistics-related local data
  • Storage location: storage on the user’s device (e.g., local database/preferences storage)

3. Permissions and Access (Device Features)

The App may use device permissions for the purposes below. Whether to allow permissions can be changed in the device settings.

  • Internet: used for network features such as displaying ads (AdMob), processing in-app purchases (subscriptions), and displaying policy/contact pages.
  • Notifications: used to send reminders for fixed expenses or alerts for spending thresholds, where enabled by the user.

4. Use of Third-Party Services (SDKs) and Information Processing

The App may use third-party SDKs to display ads, process payments, and support platform features such as local notifications. These third-party service providers may collect and process device information (e.g., advertising identifier, IP address, approximate location, in-app ad interaction information, purchase status, etc.) in the course of providing their services, and such processing is subject to each provider’s privacy policy.

  • Google AdMob (Google Mobile Ads SDK): used to provide in-app ads (banner/interstitial ads, etc.).
    Policy: Google Privacy Policy, Google Advertising (Technologies) Policy
  • Google Play Billing / Apple App Store payments (in-app subscriptions): used to provide payment features such as subscription purchases and purchase restoration. Personal information/payment information in the payment process is handled by each store operator (Google/Apple).
    Policy: Google Privacy Policy, Apple Privacy Policy

Note: If ads are not displayed due to a premium subscription, information processing via the ad SDK may be reduced or may not occur.

5. Retention and Deletion of Information

  • Locally stored information: if the user deletes the App (or deletes App data), data stored on the device may be deleted.
  • Third-party processed information: retention/deletion standards for information processed by third-party service providers follow each provider’s policy.

6. Provision (Sharing) of Information

In principle, the Company does not collect users’ personal information on the Company’s servers and does not provide it to third parties. However, third-party SDKs included in the App (ads/payments, etc.) may process information in the course of providing their services, and in such cases, the scope and processing method follow each provider’s policy.

7. User Rights and How to Exercise Them

  • Local data: since App data is stored on the device, the user can delete data by deleting the App or through device settings.
  • Personalized ads settings: the user can adjust ad personalization options through device (or Google/Apple account) settings.

8. Children’s Privacy

The App does not collect and store users’ personal information, including children’s, on the Company’s servers. However, third-party services such as ads/payments may process information, so please review each provider’s policy together with a guardian.

9. Measures to Ensure the Security of Personal Information

The Company strives to ensure that information stored locally in the App is not unnecessarily transmitted externally. Third-party SDKs are also operated in accordance with each provider’s security policies and technical safeguards.

10. Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated in accordance with applicable laws, service changes, etc., and changes will be provided in the App document.
